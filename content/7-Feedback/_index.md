> Below are my personal reflections and suggestions after completing my internship in the First Cloud Journey (FCJ) program. I would like to express my gratitude and offer constructive feedback to help the FCJ Organizing Team continue refining the program for future cohorts.

### General Assessment

**1. Working & Learning Environment** The working environment at FCJ and the office is exceptionally professional, open, and dynamic. Interns in the same cohort actively connected, shared knowledge, and supported one another throughout the process of completing technical labs. This openness greatly motivated me to adapt confidently to a real-world enterprise setting.

**2. Support from Mentors / Program Admin Team** The technical mentors and program administrators were consistently supportive, providing detailed guidance and answering in-depth questions regarding AWS infrastructure. I was particularly impressed by the mentors' approach to encouraging a problem-solving mindset—offering guidance for students to research and troubleshoot issues independently rather than simply providing direct solutions, which significantly boosted my self-reliance.

**3. Alignment Between Work and Academic Major** The FCJ training curriculum closely aligns with my Computer Networking major at university. Through a series of foundational to advanced technical labs (such as VPC, EC2, IAM, Serverless, and IaC), I not only consolidated my theoretical foundation but also gained direct hands-on experience on the actual AWS Management Console.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities** The program provided immense academic and practical value. I gained access to modern cloud technologies, learned how to optimize infrastructure costs, managed cloud resources, and, most importantly, developed teamwork skills to build a complete cloud computing project.

**5. Team Culture & Spirit** The work culture emphasizes mutual respect, knowledge sharing, and proactiveness. All members were eager to assist one another when encountering challenging labs or complex configuration errors, fostering a positive, collaborative, and cohesive learning atmosphere.

**6. Policies & Support for Interns** The program provided excellent support regarding hands-on learning environments, AWS practice accounts, and flexible scheduling to ensure students could balance their university classes. Additionally, having the opportunity to attend technical workshops and events hosted by AWS Cloud experts was a major highlight.

---

### Feedback & Responses

* **What were you most satisfied with during your internship?** What satisfied me the most was the well-structured training roadmap of the FCJ program alongside the dedication of our mentors. The practical hands-on labs helped me transform abstract networking theories into fully operational cloud infrastructure solutions.
* **What areas do you think the program could improve for future cohorts?** The program could consider adding periodic weekly group review sessions where teams can present the technical errors they encountered and how they resolved them, allowing other interns to learn from real-world troubleshooting experiences.
* **Would you recommend this internship to your friends? Why?** Absolutely. This is one of the most structured and high-quality Cloud internship programs available for Networking and IT students, effectively bridging the gap between academic knowledge and industry expectations.

---

### Suggestions & Future Outlook

* **Suggestions to Improve Internship Experience:** Provide quick reference cheat-sheets or mind maps summarizing key concepts after each major module to help interns review knowledge more easily.
* **Future Aspirations:** I hope to have the opportunity to remain connected with the FCJ community to share experiences with future cohorts, and to continue pursuing a career path as a Cloud Engineer / Cloud Solutions Architect in the future.
